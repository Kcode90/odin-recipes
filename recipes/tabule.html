Tabule Salad

The most famous Arabic salad. Tabbouleh is delicious, fresh, and has something that no other salad has: LOTS OF PARSLEY.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup of bulgur wheat
  • 2 tomatoes
  • 1 cucumber
  • 1/2 small onion
  • 1 large bunch of parsley (approx. 2 cups)
  • 1 small bunch of mint (approx. 1/4 of the parsley amount)
  • Salt
  • Lemon
  • Olive oil
  1. Hydrate the Bulgur: Place the 1/2 cup of bulgur wheat in a bowl and cover it with cold water for about 10â€“15 minutes until it softens. Drain the excess water very well and set aside. *For best results, squeeze the bulgur to remove all water.*
  2. Chop the Vegetables: Finely chop the tomatoes, cucumber, and 1/2 small onion. Set aside.
  3. Chop the Herbs: Wash and dry the large bunch of parsley and the small bunch of mint thoroughly. Finely chop both herbs. The parsley should be very abundant.
  4. Combine: In a large mixing bowl, combine the drained bulgur, chopped vegetables (tomatoes, cucumber, and onion), and the chopped herbs (parsley and mint).
  5. Dress the Salad: Add salt to taste, the juice of one or two lemons (to taste), and a generous drizzle of olive oil.
  6. Mix and Chill: Mix all ingredients gently until well combined. Cover the bowl and let the tabbouleh chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to blend.
  7. Serve: Taste and adjust seasoning (more salt or lemon juice, if needed) before serving cold.
