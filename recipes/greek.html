Greek Salad

A dish that hails straight from the sun-drenched shores of the Aegean, where the sapphire sea melts into the whitewashed villages.

Ingredients

  • 3 ripe tomatoes, cut into wedges
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and sliced
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced into rings or strips
  • 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup Kalamata olives (pitted or unpitted, to taste)
  • 4 oz block of Feta cheese (traditionally served whole or in large slices)
  • Dried oregano (for seasoning)

Dressing:

  • Extra virgin olive oil (generous amount)
  • Red wine vinegar (optional, for extra acidity)
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  1. Prepare the Vegetables: In a large bowl, combine the tomato wedges, sliced cucumber, green bell pepper, red onion slices, and Kalamata olives.
  2. Make the Dressing: In a small separate cup or bowl, whisk together a generous amount of extra virgin olive oil, a splash of red wine vinegar (if using), salt, and black pepper.
  3. Dress the Salad: Pour the dressing over the vegetables and gently toss to coat all the ingredients.
  4. Add the Feta: Place the block or large slices of Feta cheese directly on top of the dressed vegetables. *Do not toss the feta with the rest of the salad, as is traditional.*
  5. Season: Sprinkle a generous amount of dried oregano directly over the Feta cheese and the rest of the salad.
  6. Serve: Serve immediately at room temperature. The feta is meant to be broken up by the diners as they eat.
